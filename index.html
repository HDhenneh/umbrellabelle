Global Workforce Compliance

Hire anyone. Anywhere.
Without the landmines.

IR35 is the domestic compliance problem. EOR and AOR services handle the global ones — permanent establishment risk, worker misclassification, and country-specific payroll obligations.

EOR
Employer of Record

Your worker. Their legal employer.

The EOR becomes the legal employer of your worker in their country. They handle local payroll, employment taxes, statutory benefits, and labour law compliance. You retain day-to-day management. No legal entity required.

→Hiring a full-time employee in Germany, Spain, or Singapore without opening a subsidiary
→Converting an overseas contractor to employee status compliantly
→Removing permanent establishment (PE) tax risk from day one
AOR
Agent of Record

Your contractors. Managed compliantly.

The AOR sits between your business and your independent contractors globally. They handle contractor agreements, cross-border payments, insurance, and worker classification compliance. The contractor stays self-employed — but the risk is managed.

→Engaging freelancers across the US, EU, or APAC without misclassification exposure
→Consolidating contractor payments across multiple currencies from one invoice
→Ensuring contractor agreements satisfy local legal requirements

Compliance Landscape

Five risks EOR resolves — before they become problems

Your legal team will know these. Your hiring manager probably doesn't.

!

Permanent Establishment Risk

A worker operating in another country can create an inadvertent PE — triggering corporate tax liability in that jurisdiction. EOR removes the nexus by being the legal employer, not you.

!

Worker Misclassification

What you call a contractor, local law may treat as an employee — triggering backdated employment rights, tax, and fines. AOR manages classification at the contract level so the risk sits with the platform, not you.

!

Local Payroll Compliance

Every country runs different payroll tax rates, social contribution rules, and reporting deadlines. EOR handles this in-country — you pay one invoice in your currency and stay out of local tax filings entirely.

!

Data Protection (GDPR)

Processing employee data across borders requires lawful transfer mechanisms — SCCs, adequacy decisions, or BCRs. EU/UK-based EOR providers hold the appropriate legal instruments as part of their service.

!

Posted Workers (EU)

Sending UK staff temporarily to EU countries post-Brexit triggers posting obligations — notification requirements, local minimum wage rules, and social security routing. EOR or specialist advice is required for assignments over 12 months.
